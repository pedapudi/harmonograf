The double namesake

The name is two instruments at once, and both peaked in the same decade. The brand should own that: the plan and the trace.

Karol Adamiecki's harmonogram (also harmonograf), 1896 — the original interdependent-process schedule chart, invented 14 years before Gantt published his. Adamiecki's "law of harmony": matched tool speeds, coordinated timetables, real teamwork — a multi-agent coordination thesis, a century early. This is the Gantt's true name.
The Victorian harmonograph (Blackburn, Tisley), 1870s–90s — coupled, damped pendulums driving a pen: x(t) = A·sin(ft+p)·e^(−dt) per axis. Two oscillators in harmony trace a tight Lissajous figure; detuned, they precess and wander. Exactly what a multi-agent run does around its plan.

Brand rules inherited from the ecosystem language: a single line-art stroke in currentColor (round caps, never recolored), exactly one accent dot, marks that are data, not decoration — every curve below is generated live by the damped-oscillator equations, with the parameters documented. Tufte's rule applied to identity: the logo is the instrument's own output.

Option A — “the trace”

Adamped Lissajous, 3:2 pure harmonograph — the pen left running

The instrument's own drawing: a 3:2 chord with a hair of detune (3 : 2.04) so the figure precesses — it never quite repeats, like a real run. Damping pulls each pass inward; the accent dot is the pen's current position — “now” — the same dot that rides the console's time cursor. Distinctive at lockup size; at 16 px the inner passes muddy (see ladder), so it pairs with a simplified favicon cut (Option D), exactly as zicato's spiral pairs with its z favicon.

Option B — “the harmonogram”

Bthree bars and a now-rule pure Adamiecki — the schedule as emblem

Adamiecki's chart reduced to its irreducible marks: three staggered stroke-only bars (rx:3, the console's own bar radius) crossed by a vertical now-rule; the accent dot sits where the rule meets the active bar. It is the product's hero view at glyph scale — and the only option that stays fully legible at 16 px. Risk: generic (three bars say “Gantt”, not “harmonograf”) — it carries the chart lineage but drops the oscillation story.

construction: bars (6,9)→28 · (14,20.5)→40 · (6,32)→23, h 7, rx 3, stroke currentColor 2.0; now-rule x 31; dot r 2.6 at (31, 24).

Option C — “trace over plan” RECOMMENDED · LOCKUP

Cthe fusion — a drift trace threading the bars Adamiecki × Blackburn = the product

Both namesakes in one figure: quiet harmonogram bars (the plan) with a damped oscillation threading through the lanes (the execution), settling toward the center as the run converges — drift decaying to harmony, which is the entire product promise. The dot is the pen now. zicato's mark is a 1-D damped string; this is its 2-D sibling — unmistakably family, unmistakably not the same instrument. Wide aspect (3:2) makes it a natural lockup partner for the wordmark; for square favicon duty it hands off to D.

Option D — “the alpha” RECOMMENDED · FAVICON

Dclosed 2:3 Lissajous, mirrored — a lowercase α one period, undamped, flipped about the vertical axis

One clean period of the 2:3 figure, mirrored about the vertical axis — and the knot becomes a lowercase α: loop on the left, tails flicking out to the right. The harmonograph's signature shape is a letterform, and it's the letter the wordmark borrows (the “a” of graf). Crossing strokes, zero clutter: it survives 16 px and reads at a glance in a browser tab. The dot rides the lower-right tail tip — the pen at rest after the final flick. This is the favicon / avatar / app-icon cut and the chrome mark; A or C carry the large lockups.

construction: x = −19·cos 2t, y = 15·sin 3t (the mirror is the flip), t ∈ [0, 2π], closed; stroke currentColor, optical weights 1.7 / 2.6 / 3.4 by size; dot r 2.4 at the lower-right tail (cx+19, cy+15).

The wordmark

Set in the fixed brand mono (JetBrains Mono 700 — pinned, independent of any console typeface choice, zicato's rule). The distinctive move — the analogue of zicato's dotless-ı: when the wordmark stands alone, the “a” of graf is the instrument's drawing — the same Lissajous α as the mark, at x-height, dot on its lower-right tail. One alpha per lockup: beside the α mark the wordmark is plain letters — the glyph already carries the α and the dot, and doubling them is too much. hgraf is the sanctioned short form (already used by the repo's skill names), same rule.

standalone — no mark present, so the drawn “a” carries the α
beside the mark — plain letters; the glyph already carries the α + dot
short form, standalone — tab titles, CLI banners

Lockups

Horizontal: Option C + the plain-letter wordmark — the console topbar lockup (this study's own chrome now uses the α mark + plain harmonograf in place of the borrowed zıcato brand). Stacked: Option D over plain letters — README hero, social card. One alpha per lockup: the mark carries the α and the dot, so the letters stay plain; only a markless wordmark spells graf with the α. Clear space: one bar-height (7 units) on all sides; minimum mark size 14 px, below which use D only.

The accent question — which dot?

The ecosystem triad: goldfive — gold (the judge) zicato — green (the plucked note) harmonograf — “now” blue (the live cursor) zicato's own brand doc reserves the green dot for zicato. harmonograf's identity is the live view — its dot should be the color its console already spends on now: the cursor, the running stroke, the selected span.

family green — #2FA46A / #3FB87A · maximum ecosystem unity, but borrows zicato's signature
now blue — #2F7FD0 / #5EA3EC · own identity; the dot literally means “now” (recommended)

Usage — do / don't

Do

  • Stroke every mark with currentColor — it flips dark/light with the ground for free.
  • Exactly one accent dot per lockup (--hgraf-brand); it always means the pen / now.
  • One alpha per lockup. Mark present → plain-letter wordmark; wordmark standalone → the drawn “a” of graf.
  • Pin the wordmark to the brand mono; optical stroke weights by size (heavier small, lighter large).
  • Favicon = Option D (or B); lockup = C; the full trace A only at ≥ 96 px.
  • Animate only as a single draw-on (the pen drawing the figure once), gated behind prefers-reduced-motion.

Don't

  • Recolor the stroke, gradient it, or add a second accent.
  • Pair the α mark with the α wordmark — two alphas is too much.
  • Loop any animation — the instrument draws once and rests (no animation:…infinite).
  • Ship the damped trace at 16 px (it muddies — that's what D is for).
  • Use the green dot on harmonograf surfaces — green is zicato's signature.
  • Outline, shadow, or fill the marks — line art only, data-ink only.
